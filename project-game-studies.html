Card System

The cards are grouped by purpose, so the game has a readable structure instead of random effects.

Why the cards work

Each card type supports a different part of the player experience: progress, scoring, disruption, and personality. This makes the game easier to explain and more fun to play.

Progress

Course Cards

Encourage players to collect related sets and protect their progress.

Scoring

GPA Cards

Make the win condition visible and give players a clear target.

Conflict

Action Cards

Add blocking, stealing and reaction moments between players.

Theme

Adventure Cards

Bring student-life humour and make the game feel less mechanical.

Design Iteration

This section shows design thinking through before-and-after decisions instead of report-style paragraphs.

Problems Found

1
Identity cards confused players

The extra role system made the rules harder to explain.

2
Game took too long

Long playtime reduced energy and made the final goal feel far away.

3
Card text needed clarity

Some descriptions were not direct enough during actual play.

Improvements Made

1
Removed identity cards

The game became easier to learn and faster to start.

2
Adjusted GPA values

Progress became more achievable within the target playtime.

3
Redesigned action cards

Players had clearer ways to block, steal and interact.
